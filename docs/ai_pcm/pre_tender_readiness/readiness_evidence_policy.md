# Readiness Evidence Policy

This policy defines what evidence can support a pre-tender readiness status. It does not create verified legal facts and does not override GitHub main / PR / commit SHA as source of truth.

## Evidence Labels

| Label | Meaning | May Support Readiness |
|---|---|---|
| `verified` | Approved by source-of-truth packet, PR, commit SHA, or supervisor-approved evidence route | yes |
| `linked` | Linked to an approved case packet but not fully reviewed | yes, with warning |
| `placeholder` | Draft or local-only example | no |
| `unavailable` | Required evidence not present | no |
| `disputed` | Conflicting evidence exists | needs human review |
| `superseded` | Replaced by newer approved evidence | no |

## Readiness Evidence Requirements

- Demand form must identify scope, purpose, constraints, and target dates.
- Drawings / SVG must show priced boundaries and revision date.
- Photos must cover every affected work zone and key hidden-risk areas.
- Budget must separate major work packages, contingency, owner-supplied items, and exclusions.
- Material specs must define grade, finish, brand level, and substitution rule.
- Work-method notes must assign demolition, protection, hauling, installation, testing, cleanup, and restoration.
- Exclusions must be explicit enough for contractors to price the same scope.
- Payment / acceptance nodes must tie payment triggers to measurable acceptance evidence.

## Forbidden Use

- Do not treat local examples as verified case evidence.
- Do not use readiness evidence to launch formal tender.
- Do not modify formal legal contract text.
- Do not connect payment, escrow, listing fee, DB, Supabase, production AI API, or production bidding.
